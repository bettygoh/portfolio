Trust, built for 2 a.m.

A digital wallet redesign where the failure states, empty screens and scary edge cases got as much love as the happy path — because trust is won in the worst moments, not the best.

RoleLead Product Designer
Team2 designers · 5 engineers · 1 PM
Timeline4 months · 2024–25
PlatformiOS · Android
Overview

A wallet people could rely on at the moment things went wrong.

The existing wallet looked fine in a demo and fell apart in real life. A declined payment, a dropped connection, a balance that hadn't synced — each one dumped people into a dead end with no explanation and no way forward. Over four months we rebuilt the wallet around its unhappy paths first, then worked back toward the everyday. This page walks through how we got there.

The problem

When something broke, people blamed themselves — then left.

Support tickets clustered around a handful of moments: a payment that silently failed, a top-up stuck in limbo, a first launch with an empty balance and no next step. Swap in your own context: the business problem, the user pain, and what the data or research was telling you.

  • Error states were generic — “Something went wrong” with no recovery path.
  • Empty and loading screens left people unsure whether the app was broken.
  • Trust dropped sharpest right after a failed transaction.

The brief wasn't “make it prettier.” It was “make it feel safe to use when you're tired, rushed, or already frustrated.”

My role

I led design end-to-end — framing the problem, running the research, and owning the flows from first sketch to shipped build. Adjust to reflect what you actually did and where you partnered with others.

  • Facilitated discovery with support, risk and engineering.
  • Mapped every failure path and prioritised them by frequency and harm.
  • Designed the flows and the error-state system; paired daily with engineering on edge cases.
  • Ran usability sessions and iterated against what we learned.
Process

Research, then a map of everything that could go wrong.
